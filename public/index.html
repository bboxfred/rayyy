The new Phone — for the blind.

17 years after the touchscreen reshaped everything, a phone that doesn't need one. 185 million people have been waiting.

What is this?

A working demo of the next phone — built for elderly visually impaired users. The user wears it on their chest with earbuds. It listens, looks when asked, and talks back. The whole product is shaped around not needing the screen.

Persona: Auntie Mei, 72, lives alone in Toa Payoh, lost her sight five years ago. She's at AI Engineer Singapore today.
