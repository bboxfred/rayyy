What is this?

A makeshift software-and-hardware demo of a smartphone built for blind elderly users. The user wears the phone on their chest with earbuds plugged in. Rayyy listens, looks when asked, and talks back — the whole product is shaped around not needing the screen.

Persona: Auntie Mei, 72, lives alone in Toa Payoh, lost her sight five years ago. She's at AI Engineer Singapore today.

📱·🎧

phone on chest · USB-C EarPods · Bluetooth speaker

Try it yourself

Tap Start to grant mic + camera. Then tap Talk and say any of these — Rayyy is unscripted, phrasing doesn't matter:

  1. 1
    "Hello Rayyy." normal English greeting
  2. 2
    "Eh, what time is it ah?" Singlish — Rayyy will mirror your register
  3. 3
    "What am I holding?" point camera at something — vision works on demand
  4. 4
    "Where am I?" situational awareness — the family dashboard pulses too
  5. 5
    "Switch to ElevenLabs." changes voice provider mid-conversation — noticeably laggier: text routes through Gemini → ElevenLabs → MP3 (extra hop, more latency)
  6. 6
    "Call my emergency contact." emergency flow — Rayyy will tell you honestly that real telephony isn't wired in this demo
  7. 7
    "That's all." auto-disconnects so the mic stops listening

Tip: earbuds recommended. Spacebar (or the EarPods inline button) toggles Talk so you can keep your hands free.
